01 — The cost of unorganized knowledge

You have years of content.
You can't find any of it.

Every organization produces more recorded knowledge than ever — meetings, site visits, training sessions, project reviews, documentation. Almost none of it is searchable. The institutional memory exists, but only in people's heads.

i.

Search that only finds filenames

Current tools search titles and tags. They can't look inside the video, hear the conversation, or read the whiteboard. A meeting recorded three years ago is effectively lost unless someone remembers to title it correctly.

ii.

The archivist tax

Someone — usually the most expensive person in the room — is spending real time renaming files, writing descriptions, and tagging clips. This work never ends, never scales, and disappears the moment that person leaves.

iii.

Cloud AI that reads your data

Every cloud tool that offers "video understanding" requires uploading your footage to their servers. For sensitive business content — client meetings, site footage, internal strategy — this is a non-starter.

03 — How it works

Your files stay where they are.
The appliance does the rest.

Raw content remains in your existing storage — network drives, NAS, or file servers. The appliance reads these files in place, builds an understanding of what they contain, and stores that understanding in its own high-speed memory for instant retrieval.

04 — The processing pipeline

From raw content
to searchable knowledge.

Every file dropped into the watched folder moves through four stages. The whole pipeline runs overnight without supervision — by morning, yesterday's content is fully searchable.

i
Transcription

Every word, timestamped.

Audio tracks from video and standalone recordings are transcribed word by word. The system identifies different speakers, tracks language changes mid-sentence, and preserves exact timing so any moment can be located instantly.

model: whisper large-v3
precision: word-level timestamps
speakers: auto-diarization
languages: 99+ supported
# runs on local GPU
ii
Visual understanding

Scenes tagged. Text read. Objects identified.

Key moments are extracted from video and analyzed. The system records who or what is in the frame, what's happening, the environment, and any visible text — whiteboard notes, document pages, signs, screen captures. This is what makes "find the meeting with the whiteboard behind Curtis" actually work.

model: qwen2.5-vl-7b
extracts: subjects, actions, location
ocr: on-screen text + documents
output: structured tags + description
# approx. 3 min per hour of footage
iii
Meaning conversion

Language becomes searchable geometry.

Every transcript, tag, and document paragraph is converted into a mathematical representation of its meaning. This is the step that makes semantic search work — the system understands that "cost reduction" and "spending cuts" are related even though they share no words.

model: bge-m3
dimensions: 1024
context: 8,192 tokens
languages: 100+ including mixed
# open-source, no license fee
iv
Storage & indexing

Ready to answer in milliseconds.

Everything the appliance has learned is organized in a local database optimized for semantic search. Typical queries return in under a second, even against archives with tens of thousands of hours of content.

engine: postgresql + pgvector
query time: < 1 second typical
scale: millions of segments
backup: automated daily
# fully hosted on the appliance
05 — The components

Open models.
Local execution. Zero cloud.

Every AI component runs on the appliance itself. No API calls, no external dependencies, no recurring fees. Industry-leading open-source models, carefully integrated into a single orchestrated system.

Speech recognition

Whisper large-v3

OpenAI · open-source · MIT licensed

State-of-the-art transcription with word-level timestamps. Handles noisy environments, multiple speakers, and code-switching between languages mid-sentence. Trained on 680,000 hours of supervised data.

Size: ~3 GB VRAM: ~4 GB Speed: 6× realtime
Visual intelligence

Qwen2.5-VL 7B

Alibaba · open-source · Apache 2.0

Advanced vision-language model for scene understanding, object detection, OCR, and structured description. Handles documents, photographs, video frames, and infographics with equal capability.

Size: ~5 GB VRAM: ~5 GB Accuracy: SOTA
Semantic conversion

BGE-M3

BAAI · open-source · MIT licensed

Multilingual embedding model producing precise semantic representations. Supports over 100 languages in a unified space, so a question in one language can find content recorded in another.

Size: ~1.2 GB Dimensions: 1024 Context: 8K tokens
Reasoning engine

Llama 3.1 8B

Meta · open-weights · community license

Local large language model for formulating answers from retrieved content. Synthesizes information across multiple sources, quotes with attribution, and remains fully operational without internet connectivity.

Size: ~5 GB VRAM: ~5 GB Upgrade: 70B ready
